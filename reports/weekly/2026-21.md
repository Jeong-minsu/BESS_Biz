## 4. ERCOT Market Structural Update

Source: `reports/weekly/structural-update/2026-21.md` (market-analyst)

Note: ercot.com returned HTTP 403 during the structural update fetch. All ERCOT-direct data was sourced from search-indexed public records and secondary reporting.

**Key items for Week 2026-21:**

1. **PGRR145 / NPRR1325 — Batch Zero Large Load Interconnection Framework**: The dominant near-term market structure event. ERCOT filed comments May 11 incorporating stakeholder feedback. ERCOT does not support extending the March 4, 2026 RPG eligibility cutoff. Target: **June 1, 2026 ERCOT Board vote** with July 10 effective date. Board approval will determine which large loads (data centers, crypto miners) reach COD in the 2027-2029 window — directly affecting the long-run net load curve and scarcity price regime.

2. **Solar surpassing coal (2026 milestone)**: EIA confirmed 2026 is the first year solar exceeds coal on ERCOT in annual generation terms (solar ~78 BkWh vs coal ~60 BkWh). Week of May 22 saw a new weekly solar average record (12.29 GW). Texas accounts for ~40% of U.S. utility-scale solar additions in 2026 (~14 GW new TX solar this year). Midday price suppression is structural for the BESS charge window.

3. **BESS fleet capacity + GFM incentive**: ERCOT now has more than 14 GW of installed battery storage. ERCOT proposed a $1,500/MW incentive program (total cap ~$25M) for legacy BESS assets (pre-April 1, 2026 NOGRR272 cutoff) to voluntarily upgrade from grid-following to grid-forming inverters. GKS BESS holds a legacy SGIA and is not mandated to upgrade, but the incentive makes voluntary GFL-to-GFM retrofit economically viable.

4. **765-kV legislative friction**: PUCT partially approved ERCOT's West Texas 765-kV transmission plan (~$14B, ratepayer-funded). However, 25 state legislators signed a letter in May 2026 arguing the plan exceeds the authorizing bill's scope. Until the West Texas 765-kV buildout is complete, GR_WEST wind congestion constraints will remain structurally binding during high-wind periods — relevant for congestion-analyst's West export binding calls.

5. **SB 388 (Texas House, pending)**: Would require 50% of new generation after Jan 1, 2026 to be dispatchable (non-BESS). Market-moving for long-run resource mix if passed.

6. **RTC+B framework (live since Dec 5, 2025)**: Operative framework: AS Demand Curves (ASDC) replace ORDC adder; SCED co-optimizes energy and AS every 5 minutes. Current ERCOT effective summer 2026 reserve margin ~18.5% ELCC-adjusted, well above 13.75% Board minimum. No scarcity-level tightening anticipated before 2027.
