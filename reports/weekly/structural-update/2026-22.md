## 1. Market Rule & Protocol Changes

### PGRR145 / NPRR1325 — Batch Zero Board Vote: June 1, 2026 (Today)

The most significant near-term structural event for ERCOT lands on the date this report is written. ERCOT's Board of Directors is scheduled to vote on PGRR145 (Batch Zero Process for Large Load Interconnections) and companion NPRR1325 today, June 1, 2026. The June 1 target was locked in by ERCOT Chairman Gleason following the May 13 Special TAC meeting. If approved, the effective date is July 10, 2026.

- PGRR145 replaces the individual Large Load Interconnection Study (LLIS) process with a system-wide batch-based approach for new large loads (data centers, crypto miners, industrial)
- LLWG met May 21; minutes were not publicly posted as of May 25 but were expected to refine attestation/readiness requirements
- ERCOT has formally opposed extending the March 4, 2026 RPG eligibility cutoff date
- During the week of May 25-31, no new TAC or stakeholder session on PGRR145 was identified — the governance machinery paused ahead of the Board vote

WebSearch result: ERCOT confirmed the June 1 Board vote as the target for both PGRR145 and NPRR1325. TAC and PRS completed their review sequence (PRS May 6, TAC May 13). No additional protocol changes to PGRR145 were introduced during week 22.

Trading implication: Board approval on June 1 would lock the framework governing which large loads reach COD in 2027-2029. Rejection or material amendment would reset the governance timeline by months, extending uncertainty in the long-run net load and scarcity price outlook.

### NPRR1309 / NOGRR283 — DRRS (Dispatchable Reliability Reserve Service)

TAC approved NPRR1309 and NOGRR283 (Dispatchable Reliability Reserve Service) in April 2026. This new ancillary service product was not directly acted upon during week 22, but its progression toward the June 2026 Board is relevant context.

- DRRS is a new AS product requiring resources to run at least 4 hours at HSL and come online within 2 hours of instruction
- Designed to address inter-hour operational flexibility gaps that RTC+B alone does not cover
- PUCT designated DRRS as a priority in ERCOT's 2026 budget approval (November 2025)
- NPRR1309 represents the third design iteration; PUCT is expected to act post-Board

From daily reports: No DRRS-specific market events were flagged in week 22 daily briefings. The product design was in the governance handoff window from TAC to Board to PUCT during this period.

Trading implication for BESS: DRRS eligibility criteria (4-hour run + 2-hour startup) favor large, longer-duration BESS resources. GKS (100MW/200MWh = 2h) is at the margin for DRRS eligibility. If DRRS procurement becomes meaningful (estimated at $5-15/MWh in early modeling), BESS operators with 4h+ duration have a structural revenue stack advantage. Monitor capacity factor requirements in the final PUCT rule.

### NOGRR272 AGS Incentive Program — Formally Established

WebSearch result: ERCOT's $1,500/MW grid-forming technology (AGS) incentive program for legacy BESS assets was formally established on or around May 26, 2026, with a $25 million total cap and 18-month implementation window. This matches the proposal first published April 8-9, 2026.

The program:
- Targets legacy ESRs with SGIAs executed before April 1, 2026 (grid-following inverter assets exempt from the mandatory NOGRR272 requirement)
- Pays $1,500/MW for GFL-to-GFM retrofits (grid-following to grid-forming)
- Funding on order-of-receipt basis; at $1,500/MW × ~5 GW eligible legacy capacity, the $25M cap would cover approximately 16,700 MW of legacy assets — well above the eligible pool
- 18-month implementation window from the program's formal establishment date

From 2026-21 structural update: This program was identified as a key watchlist item. Its formal establishment during week 22 is confirmed. The program is voluntary and does not mandate retrofits for legacy assets.

BESS operating implication for GKS: GKS BESS was commissioned before April 1, 2026 (legacy asset). At $1,500/MW × 100 MW, the incentive value is $150,000 — meaningful for a voluntary upgrade. Management should evaluate GFM retrofit cost vs $150,000 incentive + potential future DRRS and AGS market revenue advantages.

## 4. Macro Trend Watch

**Summer 2026 Demand Outlook**: From WebSearch (natureworldnews.com, May 28, 2026): Pre-summer reporting described 2026 as "ERCOT's most consequential summer yet." ERCOT's summer 2026 peak demand range is 90,500-98,000 MW. The December 2025 CDR projects a summer 2026 reserve margin of approximately 18.3% (Protocol Prescribed peak load hour basis) — down from ELCC-adjusted 18.5% cited in the 2026-21 update, reflecting slightly different basis metrics. The CDR projects reserve margins declining year-over-year through 2030 and crossing negative in 2028 under the base scenario.

**Week 22 Load Pattern**: Daily reports show load averaging 58,412-63,283 MW across the week (Memorial Day Monday low through Sunday high). Peak load days reached 73,485-78,072 MW (HE17-18 range). The data confirms ERCOT is entering peak summer demand with load consistently at or above prior-year comparables.

**Evening Ramp Structure**: The solar-driven duck curve was structurally present every day of week 22. Net load evening ramp from HE17/18 to HE20/21 ranged from +11,488 MW (May 28, Thursday) to +16,558 MW (May 31, Sunday). This ramp is the dominant price formation mechanism and the core BESS value driver. The ramp magnitude is growing as solar capacity additions increase midday suppression.

**Natural Gas**: WebSearch (EIA/FRED data): Henry Hub spot price was $3.10/MMBtu on May 26, 2026, rising to $3.29/MMBtu on May 29. Gas was up approximately 19% for the month of May but remains approximately 4.5% below a year ago. A smaller-than-expected storage build (92 bcf vs. ~95-96 bcf forecast) reported for the week ending May 22 supported the late-May price recovery. At $3.10-3.30/MMBtu, gas-fired marginal cost is approximately $25-35/MWh depending on heat rate — consistent with the $22-35/MWh off-peak DA prices observed in week 22. The gas-price floor is an important anchor for BESS arbitrage spreads: if gas rallies to $4+ heading into summer, midday prices would rise, compressing the charge-discharge spread available to BESS.

**Wind and Solar Anti-Correlation**: The week 22 pattern of very low weekday wind (7.47 GW weekly average vs. 17.84 GW the prior week) combined with strong solar (11-12 GW weekly average) is characteristic of late-May Texas conditions. This anti-correlation is favorable for BESS: daytime solar suppresses charging prices while wind absence tightens evening markets. Week 22 BESS revenue data (GKS $5,111 on May 29) confirms this structure generates strong arbitrage and AS value when it occurs.
