### PGRR145 / NPRR1325 — Batch Zero Large Load Interconnection Framework (In Progress)

The dominant rule-change story of May 2026. ERCOT filed PGRR145 and companion NPRR1325 on March 4, 2026, proposing to replace individual Large Load Interconnection Studies (LLIS) with a system-wide, batch-based process. Key updates from the May 13 TAC meeting:

- ERCOT filed comments on PGRR145 on May 11, incorporating recent PUCT guidance and stakeholder feedback
- Revised eligibility requirements and attestation/readiness requirements for Batch Zero participation were presented
- ERCOT **does not support** extending the March 4, 2026 RPG eligibility cutoff date and opposes broad expansion of Batch Zero eligibility beyond the current queue
- Target milestone: **June 1, 2026 ERCOT Board vote** with July 10, 2026 effective date
- LLWG held a follow-on meeting May 21, 2026 (minutes not yet public as of May 25)

**Trading implication**: Batch Zero approval will determine which large loads (data centers, crypto miners) actually reach COD in the 2027-2029 window — directly affecting the long-run net load curve and the scarcity price regime.

## 2. ORDC / Scarcity Price

### No New Changes This Week

No ORDC parameter changes were announced during the May 18-24 window. The operative scarcity pricing framework remains the **RTC+B regime** launched December 5, 2025 — the largest market design change in ERCOT in 15 years.

### RTC+B Status (background for context)

Under the live RTC+B framework:
- The ORDC adder to energy prices has been removed; it is replaced by **Ancillary Service Demand Curves (ASDC)** applied to real-time AS prices
- SCED co-optimizes energy and AS every 5 minutes
- Batteries are recognized as both generators and loads in real time
- ERCOT projects >$1 billion/year in wholesale market savings

The first Triennial Reliability Standard Assessment is underway in 2026. PUCT adopted the new reliability standard rule in September 2024, mandating the first assessment cover a three-year forward window. The effective summer 2026 reserve margin (after ELCC adjustments for wind/solar/BESS) is approximately **18.5%**, well above the ERCOT Board minimum target of 13.75%. No scarcity-level tightening is anticipated before 2027 under the May 2025 CDR base case.

## 3. Generation Mix Updates

### Solar Surpassing Coal in Annual Generation — 2026 Milestone

The EIA published a forecast (May 15-19, 2026 vintage) confirming that 2026 will be the **first year solar generation exceeds coal on the ERCOT grid** in annual terms:
- Solar: ~78 BkWh projected for full-year 2026
- Coal: ~60 BkWh projected
- Texas accounts for ~40% of all U.S. utility-scale solar additions in 2026 (~14 GW of new solar in TX this year)

Weekly generation data for the week ending May 22, 2026 showed system average load of **59.09 GW** (vs 56.39 GW prior week), with solar weekly average reaching **12.29 GW** — a new weekly average record. Natural gas averaged ~22.31 GW for the week.

### Battery Storage — Capacity and Incentive Program

ERCOT now has **>14 GW of installed battery storage**. ERCOT proposed a **$1,500/MW incentive program** (April 2026, total cap ~$25M) targeting legacy BESS assets that predate the April 1, 2026 NOGRR272 grid-forming inverter requirement. The program targets approximately 5 GW of legacy grid-following capacity, encouraging voluntary GFL-to-GFM retrofit.

**For GKS BESS operations**: Grid-forming capability will become increasingly important for competitive positioning. Legacy assets (SGIAs executed before April 1, 2026) are not mandated to upgrade but the incentive program makes voluntary retrofit economically viable.

### New Project of Note — Tehuacana Creek 1 (837 MW Solar + BESS)

Expected to come online in 2026; at 837 MW it is the largest single PV project commissioning in the U.S. this year. Located in Texas. Adds to growing midday solar oversupply pressure in spring/fall.

### Data Center Load — Queue and Long-Term Forecast

ERCOT released a Preliminary Long-Term Load Forecast (2026-2032) on April 15, 2026, filed per new PUCT requirements (discussed at PUCT Open Meeting April 17):
- ERCOT's own base case: summer peak 2026 in the **90,500-98,000 MW** range (vs preliminary LTLF's aggressive 112,000 MW scenario)
- Data center demand currently ~8 GW of maximum demand against a ~94 GW system peak
- Aggressive scenario: data center demand exceeds 40 GW by 2028; ERCOT's own project is more conservative (~24 GW data center load by 2031)
- Full-year 2026 demand is forecast up ~14% vs 2025 (reaching ~425 TWh)

The wide gap between ERCOT's conservative base and stakeholder-filed aggressive scenarios is the central uncertainty in the long-run price deck.

### 765-kV Transmission Plan — Legislative Pushback

PUCT partially approved ERCOT's 260-transmission-line plan (through 2038), including three major 765-kV lines from the Permian Basin to San Antonio (cost: ~$14B, ratepayer-funded). Oncor and LCRA jointly proposed a 244-mile 765-kV line from Schleicher to Bell County on March 26, 2026. However, 25 state legislators signed a letter in May 2026 arguing the plan exceeds the original authorizing bill's scope. Construction is scheduled to begin in 2026.

**Trading implication**: Until the West Texas 765-kV buildout is complete, GR_WEST wind congestion constraints are likely to remain binding in periods of high wind output — directly relevant for congestion-analyst.
