## Key Drivers

- **Solar-driven midday suppression + steep evening net-load ramp**: With 27.5 GW solar at midday and wind dropping to ~2.5 GW, net load bottom of ~30.2 GW at HE12 gives way to a 20 GW net load ramp over HE12-HE21. This is the structural driver of the HE20-HE21 price spike window and is the day's primary trading signal.
- **Wind daytime weakness is pronounced but recovers overnight**: Wind at ~2.5 GW from HE13-HE14 means no wind backstop during solar rolloff. The GR_WEST zone (STWPF avg 4,170 MW) is the critical zone — any underperformance vs forecast during HE17-HE20 would amplify the evening price move.
- **Elevated morning outages (17.8-18.4 GW HE7-HE9)**: Resource cap out peaks at HE8 = 18,362 MW. High outage level through the morning tightens available capacity margin before solar reaches peak output. If outage levels are slow to recover, the HE7 morning price peak ($39.10) could be underestimated by the DA model.
- **Lack of data from RT Ancillary file**: The RT_Ancillary file contains no actual RT AS prices (all rows blank for DA and RT columns) — only the Smartbidder DA forecasted series in the DA_Ancillary file has data. This limits the DA-RT AS spread analysis to what the forecast model produces.

If ramp-back is slower than forecast, the HE19-HE21 price window will be sharper than $41-$46. Monitor GR_WEST STWPF vs COP_HSL divergence as the operating day progresses.

- **RT spike in HE20-HE22**: Smartbidder P(DA>RT) of 62-66% through HE15-HE21 means RT is expected to run below DA consistently, but the probability leaves room for RT spikes if load or wind deviates from forecast. Real-time watch: GR_WEST actual generation vs STWPF from HE15 onward.
