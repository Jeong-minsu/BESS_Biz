## 6. Key Risks and Opportunities

**Risk 1 — HE20–22 RT spike / AS scarcity:**
The confluence of 71 GW gross load, net load above 53 GW, GR_WEST wind decaying below 4,700 MW, and ~16.7 GW of thermal outage capacity creates meaningful scarcity risk in the 8–10 PM window. Any additional unplanned outage or demand upside (Enverus load view +2 GW) could push RT prices well above the Smartbidder DA $50–54 forecast. BESS and demand-response providers should treat HE20–22 as a priority discharge window.

**Risk 2 — AG2 wind bullishness:**
AG2 Windcast forecasts 11,320 MW average vs. Yes Energy/Enverus consensus of ~9,100–9,400 MW — a +1,900 MW gap. If AG2 is correct, the evening ramp will be substantially softened (potentially $20–30 lower at peak), and both energy revenue and AS clearing prices drop. The wind forecast divergence is the single largest swing factor for the day. Monitor 5:00 AM wind actuals in the West zone for early read.

**Opportunity — Mid-day DART short (HE10–11):**
P(DA<RT) is 65–66% at HE10–11, driven by solar-suppressed DA prices ($19–20/MWh) with potential for RT spikes from fast-ramping net load. A DART virtual short position at HB_WEST or HB_HOUSTON for HE09–11 may offer positive expected spread (RT > DA). This is a signal for the dart-virtual-trader agent.
