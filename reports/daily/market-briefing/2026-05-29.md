## Key Drivers

- **Evening net-load ramp is the dominant price driver**: Solar exits 23,212 MW at HE18 down to near-zero by HE21 while load remains above 69,500 MW. The resulting 13,500 MW net-load surge in two hours with 16,500+ MW thermal offline is the structural basis for the HE20 $60 DA spike. This is a classic late-May Texas sunset-ramp pattern.
- **High thermal outage level amplifies scarcity risk**: ~16,500-17,700 MW cap-out in the morning and ~15,500-16,400 MW through the evening peak leaves limited headroom. If any additional forced outage occurs HE19-21, RT could materially exceed DA forecasts — contra the prevailing short-DA signal. Watch ERCOT capacity monitor for any late-breaking outage additions.
- **Wind recovers strongly post-sunset**: ERCOT wind rebuilds from HE13 trough 8,120 MW to 21,319 MW by HE24. This should provide relief to the post-21:00 market, dampening overnight prices. GR_WEST overnight ramp (6,195 MW at HE14 to 13,567 MW at HE24) is the main driver — STWPF reliability for GR_WEST overnight is a key uncertainty.
- **2026-05-29 is Friday (Memorial Day weekend lead-in)**: Friday load shape typically peaks slightly earlier (HE17-18 vs weekday HE18-19). This is already reflected in the Yes Energy bidclose (HE18 = peak at 76,867 MW), but demand uncertainty is higher than a midweek day given commercial building setbacks.
