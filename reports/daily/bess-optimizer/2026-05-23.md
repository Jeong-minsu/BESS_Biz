- Overnight HE01-HE05: DA < RT (spread −$1.27 to −$0.56), P(DA<RT) 0.62-0.65 → charging in DA is favorable (buy cheap DA, avoid paying RT).

### Non-Spin (AS)
- **DA Non-Spin forecasted**: HE20 $12.52, HE21 $13.79, HE22 $13.41. No RT clearing prices available (D+1 RT AS not yet settled). These are the highest-value AS products of the day and dominate AS revenue.
- Decision: **DA Non-Spin bid at HE20-21 for 30 MW** (30 MW held offline, available for dispatch). This does not compete with the 70 MW energy DA discharge — capacity is additive.
- HE22: energy-only at 60 MW (residual capacity); Non-Spin capacity is small and market clarity lower after primary discharge window.

### ECRS
- HE21 $4.53, HE22 $4.62 — meaningful but small vs energy spread at those hours ($40, $34). Foregone when BESS is discharging energy. ECRS is only additive if BESS is idle (charging or at rest).
- Decision: **No ECRS bid during discharge hours**. If the BESS were idle at HE21 (not discharging), 100 MW ECRS at $4.53 = $453/h — but that foregoes $2,800 energy revenue. Not worth it.

### RRS
- HE20-22: $2.20-$3.08. Same logic as ECRS — inferior to energy + Non-Spin during peak discharge. Foregone.
- HE07-13: $0.45-0.67/MWh — negligible; not worth the operational complexity during charge windows.

### RegDown
- HE10-17: $1.04-2.27/MWh. BESS can offer RegDown while fully charged and idle (0 MW energy position). RegDown means BESS absorbs more energy if called, which is compatible with a full SoC only if capped. With BESS at 200 MWh (full from HE09), offering RegDown requires headroom to absorb additional charge — technically constrained.
- **Practical approach**: Offer 30 MW RegDown at HE10-17. This implies BESS would charge at up to 30 MW if called down. Given SoC is at or near 200 MWh, actual deployment risk is small and RegDown is rarely fully called during midday solar hours. Revenue is small ($31-68/h) but zero-marginal-cost capture.
- HE18-HE19: Do NOT offer RegDown — BESS should be fully available for HE20 discharge. No competing obligations.

## Risks

1. **West node congestion (GKS LMP discount vs hub, HE09-13)**: congestion-analyst MEDIUM-HIGH (25-35%) West-to-North binding during solar peak. If GKS RT falls below Smartbidder aggregate by $3-8 (congestion-analyst hub-pair basis range), the effective charging cost rises (we charge at GKS DA, which is hub-based, but settle at GKS RT). DA charge position is immunized from this — we pay DA price at GKS regardless.
2. **Non-Spin DA forecast miss**: Smartbidder forecasts $12.52-13.79/MWh at HE20-21. If actual Non-Spin clearing price is significantly lower (e.g., $5-7), the AS revenue ($790) shrinks but energy arbitrage ($3,993) remains intact. The downside is ~$400 vs a $4,700 base case.
3. **Evening wind recovery beats forecast**: If wind recovers >2 GW above STPF by HE19, net load ramp eases, HE20-21 DA spreads compress. The $44.41 DA forecast could prove high. Monitor AG2 wind updates intraday.
4. **Thermal outage surprise**: 15,154 MW capacity out HE08-10 is already priced in. An additional unplanned outage HE18-21 could spike both energy and Non-Spin above forecast — upside risk for the BESS.
5. **RegDown physical constraint**: Offering 30 MW RegDown at HE10-17 with full SoC creates a deployment risk if RegDown is called and BESS must absorb energy into an already-full battery. Recommend capping RegDown offer at 20 MW if operations team requires conservative SoC management.
6. **Solar overperformance (Enverus vs Yes Energy +4.2 GW)**: If HE12-14 solar achieves 24+ GW, midday RT prices may go slightly negative. This does not affect our DA charge position but could create small RT-vs-DA settlement differences.
