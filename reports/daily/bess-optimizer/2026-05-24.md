## Recommendation (Primary) — Stack A: AS-Enhanced DA Energy Arbitrage

### Strategy Summary

2026-05-24 is a Sunday with extreme solar (25-28 GW peak) collapsing net load to ~27.6 GW midday, followed by an 18.6 GW evening ramp — the steepest of the week. HE21 DA $65.76 is a multi-year-high-range spring price driven by P(DA>RT) 67.5% and scarcity premium on the 54 GW net load peak. HE21 ECRS $8.27 and Non-Spin $16.2 create a meaningful AS stack on top of energy discharge. The charge window is anchored to HE11-14 solar trough at DA $20.95-22.5/MWh, with early overnight fill at HE02-04 (~$28-31/MWh) to ensure SoC is available before West export congestion develops HE09+.

**Key departure from 2026-05-22 schedule**: HE21 DA energy is now the primary discharge at $65.76 (vs $45.65 on 2026-05-22). The $65.76 spike with 67.5% P(DA>RT) justifies full 100 MW discharge at HE21 without AS split. Non-Spin is layered on HE20 and HE22 as those hours carry lower energy prices ($45.58, ~$38) where AS contribution is non-trivial relative to an AS-vs-energy trade-off.

### Non-Spin AS Capacity Management (HE01-17)

The 50 MW Non-Spin offer in HE01-17 is sourced from the idle capacity headroom above the net charge position. During HE02-04 (charging), the BESS is drawing grid power but the remaining 50 MW of nameplate capacity (100 MW max - 50 MW charging) is available as Non-Spin (offline, available within 10 min). During HE05-17 (idle, not charging or discharging), the full 100 MW nameplate is technically available, but offering 50 MW (vs 100 MW) is conservative because:

1. Non-Spin DA prices are $0.4-1.9/MWh in this window — marginal value (~$20-95/h at 50 MW) does not justify aggressive capacity commitment.
2. Keeping 50 MW un-offered preserves operational flexibility if operations team has intraday needs.
3. The 50 MW figure is consistent with actual GKS Non-Spin dispatch patterns observed in 2026-05-22 P&L data (Non-Spin MW-h allocated across multiple hours at partial capacity).

During HE18-19, no Non-Spin offered — BESS needs to be fully uncommitted to execute the HE20-21 discharge without AS obligations competing for the same capacity.

During HE20: 80 MW DA energy + 20 MW Non-Spin = 100 MW total. This is the binding constraint. Non-Spin requires BESS capacity in standby; the 20 MW Non-Spin is carved from the 100 MW limit with 80 MW committed to energy dispatch.

During HE21: 100 MW full energy discharge. No AS. The $65.76 energy price makes any AS trade-off strictly inferior. 100 MW × $65.76 = $6,576 gross. 100 MW × $16.2 Non-Spin = $1,620 — but that requires the 100 MW to be offline for Non-Spin, foregoing $6,576 in energy. Energy dominates by $4,956.

During HE22: 19.5 MW tail discharge (residual SoC). No AS capacity available for discharge hours with near-zero SoC.

## DA-RT Spread Strategy

### Energy

**Signal: DA > RT dominant at HE20-22. DA < RT at HE01-19.**

DA − RT spreads (Smartbidder):
- HE01-HE19: spread is negative (RT > DA), ranging -$1 to -$5. This favors DA charging (buy cheap DA, RT settles higher — not directly profitable without DART, but confirms charging in DA is correct).
- HE20-HE22: spread turns positive (DA > RT). HE21 spread = +$16.51 (DA $65.76 vs RT $49.25) — the largest single-hour DA premium of the day. HE20 spread = +$45.58 - ~$38 RT = ~+$7-8 (estimated).

**Decision**: Full DA commitment for all discharge at HE20-22. The +$16.51 DA premium at HE21 is decisive — this is a clear short-DA / long physical delivery signal (sell into DA at $65.76, deliver energy, settle RT at ~$49).

### Non-Spin AS

- HE20 Non-Spin DA: $14.9/MWh. RT Non-Spin not available for D+1 forward view. DA is the anchoring venue.
- HE21 Non-Spin DA: $16.2/MWh. NOT offered at HE21 — energy displacement is too large ($65.76 energy vs $16.2 AS).
- HE22 Non-Spin DA: $14.9/MWh. Not offered — only 19.5 MW of SoC remains; not enough capacity to split meaningfully.

**Decision**: DA Non-Spin at HE20 only (20 MW). The DA Non-Spin prices in the $0.4-1.9/MWh window (HE01-17) add marginal revenue at ~$20-95/h and are accepted at 50 MW DA offer.

### ECRS

- HE21 ECRS $8.27/MWh. ECRS requires BESS to be idle (available to ramp into generation). At HE21 with full 100 MW energy discharge, ECRS cannot be co-offered. ECRS is only viable if BESS is idle.
- If BESS were idle at HE21: 100 MW ECRS × $8.27 = $827/h. But energy value = $6,576/h. Foregone ECRS is irrelevant — energy dominates.
- ECRS not offered at any hour in primary recommendation.

**Decision**: ECRS is a secondary product that becomes relevant only if energy arbitrage spread collapses to <$8/MWh. Not applicable here.

### RRS

- HE20 RRS $3.47, HE21 $2.74, HE22 $3.07. Same logic as ECRS. Inferior to energy at discharge hours.
- Off-peak RRS $0.1-0.7/MWh — negligible; not offered.

**Decision**: No RRS in primary stack.

### RegUp

- HE20 $3.28, HE21 $5.10. RegUp requires BESS to have headroom to increase output (i.e., currently discharging below maximum). At 80 MW discharge (HE20), theoretically 20 MW RegUp headroom exists, but that 20 MW is already committed to Non-Spin. At HE21 (100 MW discharge), no RegUp headroom.

**Decision**: No RegUp in primary stack. Operational complexity for marginal gain.

## Risks

1. **HE21 DA price miss**: If HE21 DA clears at $50 instead of $65.76 (e.g., load or wind comes in better than forecast), Stack A net drops by ~$1,576 (100 MW × $15.76). Still profitable at ~$6,988 net. The $65.76 forecast is based on Smartbidder with P(DA>RT) 67.5%; miss probability is roughly 32.5%.

2. **Non-Spin DA clearing below forecast**: Off-peak Non-Spin at $0.4-1.9/MWh is already a conservative estimate. If clearing is near zero, the $638 AS contribution from HE01-17 disappears — impact is modest. HE20 Non-Spin at $14.9/MWh (20 MW = $298) is the more material item; a 50% clearing miss here costs $149.

3. **Solar overperformance (RT negative prices HE11-14)**: If solar reaches Enverus 28.5 GW and wind is near-zero, RT prices at GKS could go negative during HE11-14. Stack A is immune — no RT charging or discharging scheduled in this window. Non-Spin DA is settled at DA prices. Downside: BESS cannot capture any cheap RT prices to boost SoC further.

4. **Starting SoC different from assumed 100 MWh**: If SoC is lower (e.g., 50 MWh from a surprise RT deployment overnight), Stack A may need to extend charging. Alternative: add HE11 or HE12 charge at DA $21/MWh for remaining SoC shortfall. Operations should confirm SoC at morning cycle start.

5. **RT spike at HE21 (upside risk)**: If RT at HE21 exceeds $65.76 (i.e., actual scarcity event beyond Smartbidder central case), the BESS's full DA commitment means no additional RT upside is captured. However, the DA settlement at $65.76 is already near the top of the expected DA distribution. Probability that RT > $65.76 at HE21 = approximately (1 - P(DA>RT)) = 32.5% — but the RT expected value given this scenario is still ~$49 (Smartbidder RT forecast). Only an ORDC-driven spike to $100+ would make withholding from DA worthwhile, and that event has P < 10%.

6. **West congestion deepening into HE15-18**: If West export binding extends past HE14, GKS DA at HE15-18 could settle below hub. Non-Spin offered HE15-17 is settled at DA prices which embed GKS-specific DA, so if GKS DA in that window is lower than hub DA, our Non-Spin capture rate would be proportionally lower. Impact is marginal ($45-95/h sensitivity).

7. **Tenaska HSL not verified**: HSL assumed = 100 MW. If actual HSL is below 100 MW (e.g., 90 MW due to heat), HE21 full 100 MW discharge is constrained. Operations must confirm HSL pre-DAM bid cutoff (10:00 CT).
