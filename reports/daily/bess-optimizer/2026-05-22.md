## Risks

**R1 — Wind underperformance HE17-HE20 (MEDIUM probability, HIGH impact):**
GR_WEST STWPF recovers from 1,280 MW (HE14) to 6,925 MW (HE20). If actual recovery is slower by 1 GW, net load at HE20 would be higher, potentially pushing DA prices above $45 and RT above forecast. This is upside risk to revenue (DA discharge would clear at higher price), but if the grid operator calls Non-Spin during HE20 and we are not positioned (idle), we miss that AS revenue.

**R2 — West export constraint deepening HE09-15 (LOW-MEDIUM probability):**
If WEST_TO_NORTH constraint binds more severely than expected, GKS nodal RT prices could be suppressed $5-20 below hub during HE09-15. This does not directly affect our DA charge position (charge cost is locked at DA clearing price). However, any RT dispatch attempted during those hours would face negative basis. Our schedule explicitly avoids RT dispatch during that window.

**R3 — Non-Spin call risk HE01-HE06 (LOW probability):**
ERCOT Non-Spin is rarely called during overnight low-load hours. However, if an overnight generation trip forces ERCOT to deploy Non-Spin, GKS would discharge up to 50 MW. Over 5 hours at 50 MW = 250 MWh potential exposure vs 100 MWh SOC. Unit would auto-exhaust at SOC=0 (battery management system protects). If called for 2 full hours (100 MWh depleted), SOC falls to 0 before HE12 charge. Recharge in HE12 still possible but SOC at start of HE21 would be ~85 MWh, reducing discharge capacity for HE22 from 100 MW to 85 MW. Revenue impact: loss of ~$1,267 (15 MW × $42.03 × 2 hours forfeited partially). Mitigant: use 30 MW Non-Spin instead of 50 MW if overnight grid conditions appear tight.

**R4 — HE21-HE22 DA price miss (LOW probability given 59-66% P(DA>RT)):**
Smartbidder P(DA>RT) at HE21 = 59.8%, HE22 = 57.9%. There is a ~40% probability that RT exceeds DA in those hours. If RT spikes (e.g., wind underperform + high load), GKS would have been better off withholding from DA and selling RT. However, the expected value calculation at 60% P(DA>RT) with a +$8 spread favors DA commitment clearly.

**R5 — HE20 opportunity cost:**
Idle during HE20 (DA $41.56) to preserve SOC for the higher HE21-22. If HE21 DA clears materially below forecast (e.g., $38 instead of $45.65), the HE20 idle was suboptimal. Conditional trigger: if Smartbidder intra-day revision drops HE21 below $43, shift strategy to dispatch HE20+HE21 instead of HE21+HE22.
