## Key Risks

1. **West wind forecast error (downside)**: If GR_WEST actual is significantly below forecast (4,300 MW at HE09), WEST_TO_NORTH binding probability rises sharply. If GR_WEST troughs below 2,000 MW, P(binding) moves to MEDIUM (20-30%) and GKS basis discount deepens to -$8 to -$20. Wind forecast uncertainty for 24-hour ahead on spring days with solar-wind anti-correlation is high.

2. **AG2 vs Enverus wind divergence**: AG2 windcast shows ERCOT aggregate at 7,906 MW at HE09 vs Enverus at 5,829 MW — a 2,077 MW gap at the same hour. If Enverus is more accurate (it uses shorter-range STPF while AG2 uses a pure windcast model), the true trough may be closer to 5,900 MW, which still places 05-26 in the LOW-MEDIUM category but toward the upper end. If AG2 is correct (~7,900 MW at trough), binding risk is even lower (LOW, ~8-10%).

3. **Evening load higher than forecast**: Yes Energy load peaks at 70,890 MW (HE17), which is high for a late-May weekday. If actual load exceeds this (heat event), the evening net load ramp could steepen and push HOUSTON_IMPORT binding probability toward MEDIUM-HIGH.

4. **Resource outage concentration at HE13**: Cap-out peaks at 17,979 MW at HE13 — the highest of the day and coinciding with the solar peak. If any additional forced outages occur during HE10-14, system headroom narrows and scarcity risk in the South increases.
