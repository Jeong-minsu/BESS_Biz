Three congestion regimes on 2026-06-01, with the **evening solar cliff as the dominant risk**:

GR_WEST wind hits daily minimum (~2,475 MW at HE13 — well below the Stage 0 elevated-West modifier threshold of 4,000 MW). Yes Energy net load trough 25,285 MW at HE10. Comparison to 2026-05-31 outlook: midday risk is lower (surplus index 1.18 vs 1.95, NL trough 25,285 MW vs 15,519 MW — significantly less extreme). Evening risk is moderate (NL ramp +13,840 MW HE19->HE21 vs +16,418 MW HE18->HE20 on 5/31). Overnight West wind risk is new for this day — 5/31 had less overnight wind concentration.

## Risk Scenarios

**Upside risk (congestion worse than base)**:
- West wind actuals come in 1,500+ MW lower than STWPF in HE09-13: GR_WEST already at 2,475 MW trough — further reduction would drive surplus concentration index above 1.3 (Yes Energy basis); WEST_TO_NORTH P(binding) escalates from ~20% to ~30-35% midday.
- Thermal outage surprise in South Texas (500+ MW forced): HOUSTON_SOUTH_IMPORT P(binding) escalates from ~30% to ~42-50%; lambda tail risk to $100-$150.
- Load exceeds 80,148 MW (heat event, early summer): Net load HE21 could approach 60,000+ MW; reserve margin compresses further.
- Overnight West wind comes in 1,000 MW above STWPF forecast in HE22-24: WEST_TO_NORTH P(binding) rises from ~22% to ~30%.

**Downside risk (congestion lighter)**:
- Solar forecast bust (-3 GW): NL trough rises to ~28,000 MW; surplus index falls to ~0.97; WEST midday risk drops from ~20% to ~12%.
- Load undershoots by 2,000 MW at HE19-21: Net load peak stays under 56,000 MW; HOUSTON_SOUTH_IMPORT risk drops from ~30% to ~18-22%.
- West overnight wind ramp fails: GR_WEST stays below 8,000 MW overnight; WEST_TO_NORTH HE22-24 risk collapses.

## Downstream Insights for Trading Agents

**IMPORTANT**: All three insights below are Stage 0 provisional, reduced-weight. Trading agents must apply independent analysis before acting.

### Insight 1 — Evening solar cliff is the primary event (HE19-21)
Solar drops ~17,154 MW in 3 hours while ERCOT-wide wind recovers only partially (+5,413 MW HE18->HE21). Net load surges +13,840 MW. This is structurally similar to typical June summer evening ramp events. HOUSTON_SOUTH_IMPORT binding probability ~30% is the highest single-constraint risk for the day. GKS BESS discharge concentrated in this window captures both energy price premium and potential congestion premium.

### Insight 2 — Overnight West wind surplus is a novel secondary risk (HE22-24)
GR_WEST wind peaks at 10,787 MW at HE24 — the daily maximum — while load falls to 63,186 MW. Cap-out declines to 12,312 MW at HE24, the lowest of the day. This creates a structural West surplus at night that is NOT present in the midday surplus index framework. WEST_TO_NORTH overnight binding (~22%) implies potential DA-RT basis compression on LZ_WEST overnight that the market may under-price. For DART: consider WEST_TO_NORTH overnight path.

### Insight 3 — Midday risk is moderate, not extreme
The surplus index of 1.18 (Yes Energy) / 0.91 (Enverus) is below the 2026-05-31 extreme (1.95). The West wind trough of 2,475 MW is low enough that the elevated-West modifier does NOT reduce risk. However, the NL trough of 25,285 MW (Yes Energy) is still above the zero-hour threshold. Base case is WEST curtailment ~20%, not HIGH. The DA market likely prices some of this midday weakness — DART short LZ_WEST HE09-13 requires careful DA clearing price check at 10:00 CT before committing.
