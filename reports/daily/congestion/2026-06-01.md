## Headline

High-confidence summer evening ramp event on 2026-06-01. Load peaks near 80,000 MW while the solar cliff (HE19-21) drops ~21,500 MW of solar in 2 hours. Net load reaches its daily maximum (57,965 MW) in HE21, well above the midday trough (25,285 MW in HE10). Three distinct congestion regimes are present:

1. **Midday over-generation window (HE09-11)**: West solar peaks ~29 GW, West wind hits daily minimum (~2,475 MW GR_WEST HE13). Potential negative congestion / curtailment constraints in West Texas.
2. **Evening ramp congestion (HE19-21)**: Solar exits fast, load stays high, thermal outage overhang (14,375 MW capacity out). South/Houston import constraints most likely to bind.
3. **Overnight West wind surplus (HE22-24)**: West wind recovers to 10,600-10,787 MW while load falls to 63-67 GW. WEST_TO_NORTH export constraints at risk.

## Risk Scenarios

**Upside risk (congestion worse)**:
- West solar forecast bust +10% (solar > 31 GW HE13) — WEST curtailment binding probability 18% → 28%. West MCC more negative (-$8 to -$12).
- Thermal outage surprise in South Texas — GKS / Houston import constraint binding probability 30% → 45%, lambda spike to $80-$130.
- Wind forecast bust (-2 GW STWPF) in HE19-21 — the only buffer for the solar cliff disappears. Net load HE21 could reach 59,500+ MW.

**Downside risk (congestion lighter)**:
- West wind ramp fails to materialize overnight — WEST_TO_NORTH risk collapses; overnight basis narrows.
- Load undershoots (cloud cover / temperature 2 degrees lower) — peak net load stays under 54,000 MW; South import constraints may not bind.
