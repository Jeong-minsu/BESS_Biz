## Headline

Three congestion themes dominate 2026-05-22:

1. **West export constraint, HE09-15 (solar peak)**: Record-high solar generation (27,523 MW peak at HE14) collapses West-zone net load to a daily minimum. With ~1,300-1,450 MW West wind still online midday, the WEST-to-NORTH export path faces its highest binding risk of the day. Smartbidder P(DA<RT) is elevated HE09-12 (0.59-0.60), meaning RT is expected to exceed DA — consistent with RT congestion relief pricing in West reducing GKS nodal RT prices below DA.

2. **Evening scarcity / South-Houston import constraint, HE19-21**: Net load surges to 50,065 MW at HE21 (highest of the day) as solar drops and load stays elevated. DA prices spike $41.6-$45.6 HE19-21. Ancillary service Non-Spin prices spike to $14-16/MWh in the same window, signaling scarcity. P(DA>RT) is strongest HE19-20 (0.66), pointing to DA-rich congestion on the import side serving Houston/South load. This is the primary constraint candidate for HE19-21.

3. **Overnight wind surplus, HE22-24**: West wind ramps from ~13,566 MW (HE22) to ~13,984 MW (HE23), pushing net load down sharply. Smartbidder P(DA<RT) climbs back above 0.52 HE22-23. West-to-North export risk re-emerges but at lower severity than midday due to lower overall load.

## Congestion Risks and Opportunities for GKS BESS

**Risk 1 — West export binding HE09-15 (PRIMARY)**
- If WEST_TO_NORTH export constraint binds during solar peak, GKS nodal RT prices may be suppressed below hub RT prices.
- GKS_BESS_RN is located in West Texas. A binding West export constraint applies a negative shift factor to the GKS bus — reducing RT prices there relative to system average.
- Magnitude estimate: unknown without PTDF, but historical West export binding has caused -$5 to -$20 GKS-vs-hub RT discounts on spring days with similar solar profiles.
- Action implication: avoid RT dispatch (discharging) at GKS during HE09-14 unless DA position is already set. Consider DA discharge bid HE09-14 at market (DA prices $24-28 are low but may be better than RT).

**Risk 2 — Morning resource outage concentration (HE08-09)**
- Resource cap-out peaks at 18,362 MW at HE08, coinciding with load ramp. This is a 2,000+ MW jump vs the day's minimum outage level.
- If unexpected generator trips occur during this window, SCARCITY pricing risk is elevated. Non-Spin already forecast $1.5/MWh but could spike materially.
- Opportunity: if GKS BESS has charging headroom from overnight hours, holding state-of-charge for HE08-09 AS dispatch (Non-Spin/RRS) has positive expected value.

**Opportunity 1 — Evening DA peak HE19-21 (PRIMARY)**
- DA prices $41-46 vs RT $34-37. DA is significantly richer. The $7-8 DA-RT spread at HE19-21 is the largest of the day.
- GKS BESS should be fully dispatched in DA market HE19-21 (discharging) if SOC permits.
- The AS market also pays well (Non-Spin $14-16) — bess-optimizer should evaluate AS vs energy tradeoff.

**Opportunity 2 — Overnight charge window HE22-24**
- Wind surge (West wind 8,600-8,940 MW) softens prices. DA prices $30-37 and falling. RT may be softer still.
- Favorable charging window. DA charge position at HE23-24 looks compelling.
