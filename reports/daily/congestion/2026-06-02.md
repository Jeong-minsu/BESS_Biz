### Risk 2: Evening NL Higher Than YE Forecast (Enverus Divergence Upside)
**Trigger**: Yes Energy shows HE21 NL at 56,761 MW — but if actual load tracks closer to the high end (Enverus HE20 already 54,694 MW), and RT wind is below AG2's 7,660 MW forecast at HE21, net load in RT could reach 58,000-60,000+ MW. This is the primary upside risk for DART HOUSTON long-RT position.

### Risk 3: West Wind Morning Underperformance
**Trigger**: AG2 ERCOT Aggregate shows wind declining from 16,267 MW (HE01) to 3,528 MW (HE11). If decline is faster (e.g., 1,500-2,000 MW at HE10 instead of 2,272 MW), solar-wind combined surplus is lower than current SCI indicates. This would reduce WEST_TO_NORTH probability, not increase it — a downside risk to the West congestion call. GKS charge in HE09-11 would be less advantageous if RT West prices rise.

### Risk 4: Cap-Out Variance at HE17-21
**Trigger**: Current cap-out 11,530-11,955 MW (HE17-21). If an additional thermal forced outage occurs overnight or morning, cap-out could drop to 9,000-10,000 MW by HE19. Combined with 79,559 MW peak load and NL ramp +10,011 MW, this would materially elevate ORDC scarcity probability and HOUSTON_IMPORT binding risk. Escalation: MEDIUM → MEDIUM-HIGH or HIGH if cap-out < 10,000 MW at HE19.
