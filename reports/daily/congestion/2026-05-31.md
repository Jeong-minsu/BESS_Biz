## Risks and Sensitivities

### Risk 1 (HIGH impact): West wind actuals come in much lower than forecast HE09-14
- Enverus West wind trough 6,898 MW at HE13 (generation), Yes Energy GR_WEST 4,537 MW at HE14.
- If actual West wind is 1,000-2,000 MW lower than forecast in the trough window (e.g., below 3,000 MW), the surplus concentration index rises toward 2.0+ and WEST_TO_NORTH P(binding) escalates to VERY HIGH (~65-75%). RT West prices could spike negative (below -$5/MWh).
- If actual is 2,000 MW higher than forecast (wind surge), WEST_TO_NORTH P(binding) drops toward 25-35%.

### Risk 2 (MEDIUM-HIGH impact): Evening load comes in 2,000+ MW above forecast
- Forecast peak 78,072 MW at HE18 (Yes Energy) / 77,551 MW HE17 (Enverus). Late May temperatures can surprise to the upside with early-summer heat.
- If load reaches 80,000+ MW at peak, SOUTH/HOUSTON IMPORT P(binding) escalates from MEDIUM-HIGH to HIGH (~45-55%).
- Conversely, if load undershoots 75,000 MW (cloud cover or demand response), evening risk drops to MEDIUM (~18-25%).

### Risk 3 (MEDIUM impact): Solar divergence — Enverus vs Yes Energy
- Yes Energy COPHSL solar: 30,257 MW at HE13. Enverus STPF: 30,727 MW at HE12. Today the two sources are closely aligned (~470 MW divergence vs 3,296 MW divergence on 2026-05-28), which increases confidence in the midday forecast.
- Residual uncertainty: solar bust of 3,000+ MW (cloud event) would raise NL trough to ~18,000-20,000 MW — still surplus-heavy but P(WEST_TO_NORTH binding) falls from HIGH to MEDIUM-HIGH.

### Risk 4 (LOW-MEDIUM impact): Cap-out at evening peak 14,639 MW — ORDC condition
- Cap-out 14,639 MW at HE19-24 is the week's lowest for this time slot. Reserve margin at HE18-20 load peak is tight.
- If a large thermal unit trips (500+ MW forced outage) between HE17-20, cap-out could drop toward 14,000 MW, materially increasing ORDC uplift and amplifying HOUSTON_IMPORT congestion signal.
