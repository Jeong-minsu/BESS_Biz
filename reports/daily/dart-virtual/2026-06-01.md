## Risks

1. **Enverus upside scenario (net load +2,355 MW avg vs Yes Energy)**: If Enverus is correct, HE21 reserve margins narrow further, increasing the probability of ORDC adder activation and RT prices printing above DA-spread forecasts — which would be adverse for the SHORT DA positions. The SHORT DA position wins if RT stays below DA at clearing. An Enverus scenario pushes RT toward DA convergence, shrinking the spread. Partial risk at HE20-21.

2. **Smartbidder DA overestimation bias at HE21 ($52.26)**: Market-analyst estimates the actual DAM clearing for HE21 will be $44-47 after applying the 10-15% overestimation discount. The SHORT DA bid is placed at $52.26 (Smartbidder nominal). If the actual DA clears at $44-47, the realized spread is $2-5/MWh vs the forecast $10.10. The SHORT DA position still wins directionally (DA > RT) but P&L is smaller. The bias correction is applied to E[gross] but not to the bid price itself — the bid at $52.26 ensures the position clears.

3. **Wind forecast bust at HE17-21 (RT spike risk)**: GR_WEST wind is forecast to recover from 2,475 MW (HE13 trough) to 9,085 MW by HE21. If actual wind is 2,000+ MW below STWPF during HE18-21, RT prices could spike above DA during the ramp (adversely impacting SHORT DA positions). Wind sources (YE and Enverus) are closely aligned today (−131 MW gap), limiting this uncertainty, but HE18-19 are most exposed.

4. **HOUSTON_SOUTH_IMPORT congestion intensification**: Congestion-analyst estimates P(binding)~30% at HE19-21 for this constraint. If a South Texas thermal unit trips (500+ MW), P escalates to 42-50%. Binding HOUSTON_SOUTH_IMPORT creates a positive MCC at HB_HOUSTON — directionally favorable for SHORT DA at HB_HOUSTON (our node), but the magnitude uncertainty is large (Stage 0 heuristics, MCC error factor 2-3x). The congestion effect could augment or partially offset the hub-level spread.

5. **HE18 borderline entry**: The HE18 25 MW MED entry fails the strict $4/MWh spread threshold. If spread is indeed ~$2.40 and P is 62% (coin-flip range for this spread level), this position may realize a near-zero outcome in either direction. Maximum downside on this position: $60 (25 MW × $2.40/MWh). Monitor DA clearing after 10:00 CT.

6. **Monday post-holiday commercial load return uncertainty**: Market-analyst notes June 1 is the first full commercial weekday of summer. If commercial load returns slower than expected (some firms still on holiday schedule), HE17-19 load could print 1-2 GW below the 80,148 MW forecast, softening the peak and reducing DA. This would compress the HE20-21 spread but not eliminate it — the solar-exit-driven deficit at HE21 is structural regardless of whether load peaks at 78 GW or 80 GW.

7. **Overnight long DA HE01-02 inversion risk**: Prior cycle (2026-05-31) showed HE24 with RT > DA (inverted from the model direction). Similar inversion risk exists at HE01-02 if overnight load is softer than forecast or wind ramps even higher than the already-elevated 16,418 MW. The 25 MW minimum size limits exposure. Maximum downside: $39-42 per position ($78-84 combined).
