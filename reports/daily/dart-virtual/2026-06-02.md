**Impact on DART position at HE20**: Both scenarios support the SHORT DA thesis at HE20. If Enverus is correct, the HE20 supply deficit is even tighter, which strengthens P(DA>RT). If YE is correct, HE20 is still tight at 54,894 MW net load with 12,086 MW offline. The solar divergence creates asymmetric upside for the HE20 SHORT DA position — it wins under both scenarios, potentially by more if Enverus is correct.

**Risk caveat**: If YE's 4,764 MW solar estimate is correct AND the DA clearing price reflects DA-bidders pricing in that solar cushion, the DA-RT spread at HE20 could be narrower than the inferred $8/MWh. This is the primary spread-compression risk for HE20.

1. **Smartbidder data absent (primary operational risk)**: This cycle's price view is supply-demand inferred without Smartbidder DA/RT price forecasts or P(DA>RT) probabilities. The 0.85 confidence discount captures a blanket uncertainty increase, but the individual hour P estimates carry ±5-6pp uncertainty vs the normal ±3-4pp. Before the 10:00 CT DAM bid cutoff, if Smartbidder data becomes available, recalibrate all P estimates and revisit HE18 (P ~59%) and HE01-02 (E[gross] sub-threshold) for possible reinstatement.

2. **HE20 solar uncertainty (Enverus vs YE divergence, −4,223 MW)**: The direction of impact is asymmetric — Enverus scenario (lower solar) strengthens the HE20 SHORT DA case. However, if YE's 4,764 MW holds and the DA market priced in that solar buffer, the actual spread may be compressed vs the $8/MWh forecast. Monitor actual solar ramp-down trajectory intraday.

3. **Weaker wind (9,378 MW avg vs 11,103 MW yesterday)**: Lower wind average (−1,725 MW) means more thermal dispatch required system-wide. This is net bullish for evening DA prices (tighter supply) and supports the SHORT DA thesis by keeping DA elevated. However, if wind under-delivers further in the HE18-21 window (RT spike scenario), RT could approach or exceed DA, adverse for SHORT DA positions. GR_WEST wind recovery pace at HE18-21 is the key variable.

4. **Improved thermal availability (12,086 MW avg cap-out vs 13,907 MW yesterday)**: An additional ~1,800 MW of thermal is available today. This is the primary structural bearish offset vs yesterday. The most significant hour of impact is HE21 — more thermal reduces ORDC adder probability and may cap DA clearing below the June 1 equivalent. The net load at HE21 (56,761 MW) is still 3+ GW above any recent daily average, so thermal improvement is a compression factor, not a reversal.

5. **HOUSTON_IMPORT congestion escalation risk (Risk Flag 2 from congestion-analyst)**: If RT net load exceeds 58,000 MW at HE21 (lower-than-forecast wind scenario), HOUSTON_IMPORT escalates from MEDIUM (~22%) to MEDIUM-HIGH. This creates a positive MCC premium at HB_HOUSTON — directionally favorable for SHORT DA positioning at this node. The risk is the asymmetric upside scenario, not adverse.

6. **HE19 sizing at MED (not HIGH) — opportunity cost risk**: If the HE19 spread realizes at $6-8/MWh (above the $4/MWh inferred value), the MED 25 MW sizing costs ~$50-100 in opportunity profit vs HIGH 50 MW. This is an accepted trade-off given no Smartbidder confirmation.

7. **Tenaska data unavailable (9th consecutive cycle)**: Model calibration remains frozen. The +20% E[spread] bias correction and 0.85/0.95 confidence discount framework are the best available proxies, but without RT actuals, hit-rate tracking and win:loss ratio confirmation are impossible. Tenaska PTP Ascend IP whitelist resolution remains top process priority.
