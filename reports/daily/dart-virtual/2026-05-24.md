## Analytical Framework

### Spread = DA − RT (project-wide sign convention)

- Positive spread (DA > RT) → Short DA / Long RT signal
- Negative spread (DA < RT) → Long DA / Short RT signal

### Sizing rules (v0 model)

Full size for HIGH tier is set at 50 MW (up from 25 MW on weekdays) because larger spreads justify larger positions; MED tier sized at 22 MW (conservative given reduced market depth confirmation).

## Why (Position Rationale by Group)

### HE10-12 Long DA at HubN — Solar Suppression + Congestion Arbitrage

Three-signal confirmation:
1. **Probability**: P(DA<RT) = 62-63% (above MED 60% threshold). Market-analyst confirms HE09-12 RT > DA with P(DA<RT) 60-63%.
2. **Point spread**: DA forecast ~$21-22, RT forecast ~$24-25 → spread magnitude -$3.5/MWh, well above $1.00 MED minimum.
3. **Congestion overlay consistent**: WEST_TO_NORTH constraint is expected to bind HE09-14 (P 35-50%, congestion-analyst). Under this binding, the North hub (HubN) is the receiving end and receives a premium in RT relative to DA. This is consistent with RT > DA at HubN — the constraint lambda flows to the receiving node. Trading Long DA at HubN aligns with the congestion direction.

**Key assumption**: LONG DA at HubN is settled against HB_NORTH. If executed at HubW (West hub), the congestion suppresses RT at HubW, potentially inverting the thesis. Node selection = HubN is critical.

**Skipped HE09, HE13**: HE09 is the early transition hour (P=60%, spread starting to develop, congestion probability just rising — confidence insufficient for entry without further signal buildup). HE13 is the first recovery hour (P falling to 61%, spread compressing, load recovery underway).

### HE19-22 Short DA at HubH — Evening Ramp + Sunday DAM Structural Overprice

Four-signal confirmation:
1. **Probability**: P(DA>RT) = 66.7-68.4% across all four hours — highest sustained P block of the day. All four hours clear HIGH tier (>= 60%).
2. **Point spread**: DA-RT = +$2.54 to +$16.51 — positive (DA > RT) per Smartbidder. All four spreads exceed the $2.00 HIGH minimum.
3. **Congestion overlay consistent**: Houston import constraint binding HE19-22 (P 35-45%). When Houston import constraint binds, North-side (HubN) and Houston-side (HubH) RT prices reflect the import stress but often clear below the DA price which priced in worst-case scarcity. The constraint drives DA prices up (scarcity insurance buying in DAM) more than it drives up RT settlement. Short DA wins.
4. **Sunday structural alpha**: Market-analyst explicitly notes DAM Sunday liquidity is lower, causing DA to systematically overprice relative to RT in evening peak hours. This is a recurring structural edge on Sunday flows.

**Node HubH selection**: Congestion-analyst identifies Houston/South import as the primary evening constraint. HubH (HB_HOUSTON) is the import-receiving node. Under this constraint, HubH DA prices carry a premium (load pays scarcity). RT at HubH may clear lower than DA as fast-ramp resources clear into the constraint. HubH is the higher-alpha node for Short DA in this regime vs HubN.

**HE21 sizing justification**: $16.51 spread with 67.5% P is the highest risk-adjusted EV of the year-to-date cycle. Maintaining 50 MW (not reducing) is warranted by the triple signal: large spread, high P, Sunday structural edge. The tail risk of RT spike above $65.76 is acknowledged and limited to 50 MW position.

## Risks

### Risk 1 — HE21 RT Spike (Primary tail risk)
**Trigger**: Evening ramp faster than forecast. Enverus net load 54,446 MW + resource cap out 15,100 MW = 69.5 GW effective supply requirement with limited headroom. If a generator trips HE18-20, ORDC can move RT from $65 to $100-200+. Under this scenario, Short DA at $65.76 loses (RT > DA).  
**Probability**: Congestion-analyst puts "generator forced outage during ramp HE18-20" as LOW-MEDIUM scenario — unforecastable by design.  
**Max loss at HE21**: (RT - $65.76) × 50 MW. At RT = $150, loss = ($84.24) × 50 = -$4,212. At RT = $200, loss = -$6,712. These are tail scenarios but real.  
**Mitigation**: 50 MW limit (not 100 MW). Monitor RT 5-min prices HE18 after 17:00 CT; if RT is already trending above DA by HE18, consider reducing HE19-21 exposure.

### Risk 2 — Wind Forecast Divergence at HE19-21 (Medium risk)
**Trigger**: Enverus wind HE24 = 10,870 MW vs Yes Energy = 8,219 MW. If actual wind recovers faster than Yes Energy forecast during HE19-21 (e.g., hits 7-8 GW by HE21), RT prices could fall more than the $49.25 Smartbidder RT forecast — actually FAVORABLE for Short DA. However, if Enverus is correct and wind is abundant, the net load ramp may be less severe (HE21 net load = 54,446 MW - 2,651 MW extra wind = 51,795 MW), reducing RT spike risk. Wind upside is mostly benign for our Short DA thesis.  
**Counter-risk**: If wind is LESS than Yes Energy (e.g., GR_WEST stays at 1,500 MW through HE20-21), RT spike amplifies. GR_WEST at HE21 Yes Energy = 3,531 MW — if actual is 1,500 MW, that is a -2,031 MW shortfall that tightens supply.

### Risk 3 — WEST_TO_NORTH Congestion Timing Error (HE10-12 Long DA)
**Trigger**: If WEST_TO_NORTH constraint does not bind or releases earlier than HE10-12 (e.g., wind was underforecast and actual GR_WEST is 1,500 MW instead of 600 MW at HE10), then the spread compression that justifies LONG DA at HubN may not occur. P(DA<RT) drops, spread narrows.  
**Probability**: Congestion-analyst notes "Wind forecast error (Enverus 348 MW realized closer to 1-2 GW) → WEST_TO_NORTH P(binding) -8 pp." At 22 MW MED size, max loss per hour = $3.50 × 22 MW = $77.  
**Mitigation**: Small (22 MW) MED sizing limits loss. HubN node selection insulates from worst-case West-side RT suppression.

### Risk 4 — Sunday DAM Thin Volume Causing Fill Issues (Execution risk)
Reduced DAM participation on Sundays means DART virtual bids may partially fill or clear at prices away from forecast. Monitor bid clearing confirmation after 10:00 CT DAM results.

### Risk 5 — Enverus vs Yes Energy Solar Discrepancy
Enverus peaks at 28,486 MW (HE14); Yes Energy at 25,678 MW (HE15). A 2,807 MW discrepancy at HE10 is material for WEST_TO_NORTH binding timing. If actual solar is below Enverus (closer to Yes Energy), the net load trough may occur at HE12-13 instead of HE10 — our LONG DA entry at HE10-11 might trade into a slightly weaker signal. Still, both sources agree on the direction; timing shift of 1-2 hours is the risk, not direction reversal.
