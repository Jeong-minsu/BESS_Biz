## Key Risks

1. **Wind forecast bust (upside) — primary RT spike risk for Short DA**: The AG2 vs Yes Energy/Enverus wind gap is 1,900 MW at the system level. If AG2 is correct (11,320 MW average vs Yes Energy's 9,418 MW), the evening price ramp is substantially muted — but this actually HELPS Short DA (RT suppressed below DA). The directional risk for Short DA is on the downside: if actual wind is significantly BELOW even Yes Energy (e.g., a cold front delays West zone wind ramp), RT at HE20-21 could spike above $50-53 DA levels. Monitor 05:00 CT GR_WEST actuals; if wind is tracking below 3,500 MW at HE09 (threshold for elevated RT spike risk), reduce HE20-21 to MED size before 10:00 CT DAM cutoff.

2. **Thermal outage concentration at HE20-21**: Resource cap-out sits at 16,726-16,835 MW during HE18-23, with peak outage 17,979 MW at HE13. Elevated thermal outages with net load at 53,062-56,051 MW leaves thin reserve margin. Any unplanned outage during HE19-21 could trigger ORDC scarcity pricing (5-min RT >> $100/MWh). This is the primary tail risk for the Short DA block. One ORDC event at HE20 or HE21 with 50 MW exposed = full position loss plus adverse spread. The AS spike signals (Non-Spin $17, ECRS $11 at HE21) are precisely warning of this scenario.

3. **HOUSTON_IMPORT constraint non-binding**: The MEDIUM probability (20-28%) means the constraint does NOT bind 72-80% of the time. In non-binding hours, HB_HOUSTON's positive basis premium vs HB_NORTH disappears. The Short DA thesis still holds on DA-RT direction alone (market-analyst P=66-67%), but the expected spread magnitude shrinks. E[spread] estimates already conservatively exclude the full +$12/MWh conditional lambda; the +20% bias correction partially compensates.

4. **Long DA solar block — WEST_TO_NORTH non-binding**: P(binding) = 12-18% means the constraint does not bind in 82-88% of scenarios. The Long DA thesis for HE10-11 rests primarily on market-analyst P(DA<RT)=65-66% rather than congestion. If RT tracks with DA in the HE10-11 window (no spread), max loss = $500 gross for the two-hour block at 25 MW each (assuming -$1/MWh adverse spread). Downside is limited given small absolute size; upside is meaningful if WEST_TO_NORTH binds (lambda $10-30 conditional).

5. **No Smartbidder P(DA>RT) data — fifth consecutive day**: All P estimates are first-principles reconstructions using Smartbidder DA price levels, Yes Energy/Enverus/AG2 fundamentals, and market-analyst briefing P citations. The market-analyst's P citations (65-67% for HE20-22, 65-66% for HE10-11) substantially improve confidence relative to prior cycles where no P anchor existed. Confidence discount 0.80 maintained; if Smartbidder data becomes available before 10:00 CT, update and re-evaluate.

6. **Enverus load outlier (+1,902 MW vs consensus)**: Enverus forecasts 60,512 MW average vs Yes Energy/AG2 consensus at ~58,610-58,618 MW. If Enverus's higher load view is correct, the HE20-21 net load peak moves from 53,062 MW (Yes) to 56,051 MW (Enverus) — adding further stress to the evening window. This is upside for Short DA positions (more scarcity insurance in DA) but also elevates the ORDC tail risk.
