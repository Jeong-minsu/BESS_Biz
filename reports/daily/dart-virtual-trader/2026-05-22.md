## Position Rationale

### SHORT DA bias HE20-23 (PRIMARY thesis)

The evening net-load ramp is the structural driver. Between HE12 and HE21, net load climbs ~20 GW as solar drops from 27,523 MW to 350 MW while load stays elevated at 63,521 MW. DA prices reflect this scarcity pre-emptively: HE20 DA = $45.65, HE21 DA = $42.03. The RT market, operating on 5-minute intervals and catching some of the wind ramp benefit (ERCOT wind reaches 12,542 MW by HE21), tends to price less aggressively than the day-ahead unit commitment.

Smartbidder P(DA>RT) is 66.2% at HE20 and 62.4% at HE21 — the two strongest DA-expensive signals of the day. The forecasted DA-RT spread is +$7.03 and +$8.00 respectively. The AS market Non-Spin price of $14-16/MWh at HE20-22 is consistent with scarcity that is being priced into the DA market but that the 5-min RT market may not fully replicate.

HE22 and HE23 carry the SHORT DA thesis forward as the spread remains positive (+$5.81, +$4.66) and P(DA>RT) is 59-60%, sufficient for half-size medium conviction.

### LONG DA midday HE12-14 (SECONDARY thesis)

Solar generation of 25,111-27,523 MW during HE12-14 suppresses the DA market, with DA prices $26-29 against RT prices $27-30 (RT forecast ~$1.3-2.2 richer). P(DA<RT) reaches 60.1% at HE12 and remains 57-59% through HE14. The West export constraint (MEDIUM-HIGH qualitative probability from congestion-analyst) is a risk to GKS nodal pricing but for a hub-level financial DART virtual position, the West-to-North basis effect is secondary. The hub-level DA-RT differential drives the DART virtual settlement.

HE12 carries the highest probability (60.1%); HE14 carries the largest spread (-$2.22). Half-size (12 MW) appropriate given W:L ratios of 1.36-1.51, which are in the medium conviction band.

## Signal Anomalies and Conflicts

**HE16-17 (FLAT — critical anomaly)**
Smartbidder P(DA>RT) = 64.6% and 62.1% for HE16-17, which would normally indicate medium conviction SHORT DA. However, the Smartbidder central price forecast shows DA $31.36-31.48 BELOW RT $33.19-33.59 (spread -$2.23 and -$1.71). This is a rare but important internal inconsistency: the probability model assigns >60% likelihood to DA outpricing RT, yet the same model's point forecast has RT as more expensive.

Two interpretations:
1. Fat upper tail: the probability model integrates scenarios where DA spikes significantly, pulling P above 50% even though the median outcome has RT > DA.
2. Model instability: calibration artifact in the transition from solar to evening ramp hours.

Per SKILL.md guidance (Smartbidder DA-RT probability has weak standalone alpha), and lacking our own quantile model to resolve this, we skip HE16-17. Trading the probability alone against a conflicting central forecast creates unquantifiable expected-value uncertainty.

**HE19 (FLAT — spread data discrepancy)**
P(DA>RT) = 66.2% (tied for day-high with HE20) but the Smartbidder LMP5 point forecast is $31.90 vs DA $41.56, implying a +$9.66 spread in the energy forecast CSV. However, the market-view briefing references RT $34.52 for HE19, a $2.62 discrepancy from the LMP5 series in the Energy Price Forecasts CSV. This discrepancy makes the spread estimate unreliable. Skip until data source reconciliation is completed.

## Risk Points

1. **Wind underperformance HE17-21 (PRIMARY RT spike risk for SHORT DA)**
   GR_WEST wind forecast falls to 1,280 MW at HE14 and ramps back to 5,727-7,941 MW HE19-21. If the ramp-back is slower than forecast, RT prices at HE20-21 could exceed the current $37-38 forecast, compressing or erasing the DA-RT spread. This is the single largest risk to the HE20-21 HIGH conviction positions. Monitor GR_WEST STWPF vs actual from HE15 onward; if actuals trail forecast by >1,000 MW by HE17, the RT spike risk increases materially.

2. **AS market Non-Spin spike amplifying RT (HE20-22)**
   Non-Spin at $14-16 signals scarcity. In ORDC-driven pricing, scarcity adders can lift RT settlement prices above the energy-only forecast. Our SHORT DA positions at HE20-22 are sized to absorb up to ~$5-7 of RT overshoot before the position turns net-negative (spread would need to compress from +$7-8 to near zero).

3. **West export binding HE09-15 (risk to LONG DA positions HE12-14)**
   Congestion-analyst flags MEDIUM-HIGH probability of WEST-TO-NORTH export constraint binding. If this constraint suppresses West-node RT prices, the hub-level DA-RT spread could be affected depending on flow-weighted hub composition. Positions at HE12-14 are half-size precisely to limit exposure. If congestion binding is severe and the hub RT price drops below DA, LONG DA positions would lose.

4. **Outage persistence HE07-09**
   18,362 MW resource cap-out at HE08 is elevated. If outages persist into HE10-12, it tightens reserve margin during solar transition and could lift RT prices mid-morning, which would widen the LONG DA spread favorably for HE12-14 positions. This is a upside scenario for midday LONG DA, not a primary risk.

5. **Single-source data (no AG2 / Enverus cross-check)**
   Market-analyst flagged that AG2 and Enverus were not fetched this cycle. Load forecast confidence is single-source only (Yes Energy BidClose). If actual load deviates >2,000 MW from the 57,265 MW average, all price forecasts are stale. Treat all positions as carrying wider-than-normal confidence intervals.
