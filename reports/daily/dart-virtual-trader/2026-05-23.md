GR_WEST wind is forecast at 2,905 MW (daily trough) at HE14 and expected to recover by HE20-21. If wind recovery is slower than STWPF forecast — a persistent pattern on spring days with anti-cyclonic flow — RT prices at HE18-22 could overshoot the $28-36 range. The SHORT DA positions lose if RT rises above DA ($31.80-$44.40). The spread buffer on HE20 (+$7.90) provides substantial cushion; HE18 (+$3.10) is most vulnerable.

Trigger to watch: if GR_WEST actual generation at HE16-17 is more than 1,000 MW below STWPF, RT spike risk at HE19-21 increases materially.

### 2. West export congestion suppressing midday RT — FLAT decision validated
Congestion-analyst flags MEDIUM-HIGH (25-35%) probability of WEST_TO_NORTH 345 kV binding at HE09-13. If binding, West-node RT prices fall below hub, but more importantly for hub-level DART: the binding constraint can lower aggregate ERCOT RT prices in the midday window. This supports the FLAT call for HE09-12 even at hub level.

### 3. HE23 wind ramp uncertainty — validates FLAT
AG2 Windcast shows ERCOT wind recovering to 10,745 MW by HE23, but Yes Energy shows a more moderate recovery. If wind ramps strongly at HE22-23, RT at HE23 could fall further below DA, which would mean P(DA>RT)=66% is correctly predicting the outcome — and our FLAT decision would leave money on the table. However, the point-forecast spread of -$1.00 (RT > DA) cannot be ignored given the Smartbidder weak standalone alpha rule.

### 4. Thermal outage risk
Resource cap-out of 14,878-15,154 MW is persistent. An unplanned unit trip during HE18-20 (the ramp window) would tighten reserves and amplify RT scarcity prices. This would work AGAINST SHORT DA positions at HE18-20 (higher RT → spread compresses). Not the central case, but tail risk worth noting.

### 5. Solar forecast uncertainty (Enverus vs Yes Energy +4.2 GW gap)
If solar is stronger than Yes Energy (i.e., closer to Enverus's 24,287 MW), midday net load falls lower, potentially amplifying the West export congestion. RT at midday could go negative or very low. FLAT on HE09-14 is the appropriate risk-avoidance posture under this uncertainty.
