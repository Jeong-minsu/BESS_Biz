## Headline

Three congestion themes for 2026-05-25 (Monday). The fundamental character of this day differs materially from 2026-05-24 (Sunday) on the key West wind variable.

1. **Solar surge + moderate West wind midday (HE09-14) — West export constraint signal present but significantly attenuated versus prior day**: Yes Energy COPHSL solar peaks at 29,211 MW at HE13 (near the weekly maximum). However, GR_WEST wind holds at 2,749-3,089 MW through HE10-13 (vs a near-zero 600 MW trough on 05-24). Enverus STPF trough is 3,873 MW at HE10 — marginally below the 4,000 MW heuristic threshold but not near-zero. Net load troughs at 20,931 MW (Yes Energy, HE10) and 24,855 MW (Enverus, HE10). WEST_TO_NORTH 345 kV binding probability is MEDIUM, materially lower than the HIGH signal on 05-24.

2. **Monday load surge — 72,614 MW gross load peak at HE17 — largest load day in this data series**: Monday returns full commercial and industrial load. ERCOT gross load peaks at 72,614 MW at HE17, approximately 8,100 MW (+12.6%) above Sunday's peak. This is the primary structural difference from the prior day. High Monday load simultaneously (a) absorbs more West renewable surplus locally, reducing West export stress, and (b) creates a larger thermal generation requirement that stresses North and Houston import corridors during the evening peak. HOUSTON/SOUTH IMPORT constraint binding probability is HIGH.

3. **Evening ramp + solar collapse (HE19-21) — the primary dollar-value congestion window**: Net load ramps from 35,412 MW (Yes, HE17) to 52,767 MW (Yes, HE21), a +17,355 MW swing. Enverus shows a +16,653 MW ramp. Solar collapses from 17,783 MW at HE19 to 415 MW at HE21. Simultaneously, resource cap out is elevated at 17,294-17,311 MW through HE17-21. The combination of Monday-scale load, solar exit, and constrained generation headroom makes the HE19-21 window the highest-congestion-risk window of the day.
