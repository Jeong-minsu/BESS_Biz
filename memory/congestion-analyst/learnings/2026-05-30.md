## 3. Methodology Observations and Calibration Updates

### 3a. Surplus concentration index — behavior at extremes confirmed

The 2026-05-31 forecast was the highest surplus concentration index day of the tracked sequence (1.95 Yes Energy, 1.80 Enverus). The progression across the week shows:

| Date | Surplus index | GR_WEST trough | NL trough | Call |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-05-24 | 1.056 | ~600 MW | ~27,000 MW | HIGH |
| 2026-05-28 | 1.014 | 727 MW | ~29,700 MW | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| 2026-05-29 | 1.151 | 6,195 MW | 23,827 MW | LOW (West wind offset) |
| 2026-05-31 | 1.80-1.95 | 4,537 MW | 15,519-17,050 MW | HIGH (modifier partial) |
| 2026-06-01 | 0.91-1.18 | 2,475 MW | 25,285-32,347 MW | MEDIUM (~20%) |

This week confirms the index is directionally robust. The West wind modifier (>4,000 MW → reduce one step) needs refinement for the 4,000-6,000 MW range: at 4,537 MW (2026-05-31) the call remained HIGH; at 6,195 MW (2026-05-29) the call was LOW. The threshold is somewhere between 4,537 and 6,195 MW for the modifier to fully suppress the WEST_TO_NORTH signal given high surplus index.

**Proposed calibration refinement**: Apply the West wind modifier at three levels:
- GR_WEST trough < 3,000 MW: no modifier (full HIGH stands)
- GR_WEST trough 3,000-5,000 MW: reduce by half-step (VERY HIGH → HIGH, or HIGH → MEDIUM-HIGH)
- GR_WEST trough > 5,000 MW: reduce by full step (HIGH → MEDIUM)
- GR_WEST trough > 7,000 MW: reduce by two steps (HIGH → LOW-MEDIUM)

This would produce: 2026-05-31 (4,537 MW → half-step, HIGH) and 2026-05-29 (6,195 MW → full step, MEDIUM — close to the LOW call made). Better than the binary 4,000 MW threshold.
