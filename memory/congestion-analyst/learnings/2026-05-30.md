## 3. Structural Observations and Calibration Updates

### 3a. Surplus concentration index as primary heuristic — confirmed, range extended

The 2026-05-31 D+1 forecast produced the highest surplus concentration index in the tracked sequence (1.80-1.95), substantially above the 2026-05-24 HIGH reference (1.056). The heuristic progression across the week:

| Date | GR_WEST trough | Solar peak | NL trough | Surplus conc. index | Call |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-05-24 | ~600 MW | ~28,500 MW | ~27,000 MW | 1.056 | HIGH |
| 2026-05-25 | ~2,749 MW | ~22,000 MW | ~33,000 MW | 0.667 | MEDIUM |
| 2026-05-26 | ~4,300 MW | ~23,305 MW | ~29,114 MW | 0.800 | LOW-MEDIUM |
| 2026-05-27 | 628 MW | 20,425 MW | 36,175 MW | 0.565 | MEDIUM |
| 2026-05-28 | 727 MW | 30,118 MW | 29,700 MW | 1.014 | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| 2026-05-29 | 6,195 MW | 27,423 MW | 23,827 MW | 1.151 | LOW (West wind offset) |
| 2026-05-31 | 4,537 MW | 30,257 MW | 15,519 MW | **1.950** | HIGH |

The 2026-05-29 call (LOW for WEST_TO_NORTH) despite a surplus concentration index of 1.151 illustrates that when West wind is high (6,195 MW vs ~600-727 MW on HIGH days), the congestion risk on WEST_TO_NORTH specifically is reduced even if the systemwide NL trough is low. The West wind is the primary transmission driver for WEST_TO_NORTH, and a high NL surplus can be accommodated by the West region itself when local wind generation is elevated. For 2026-05-31, West wind trough is 4,537 MW — elevated but not nearly as high as 2026-05-29 (6,195 MW), while NL trough is 15,519 MW vs 23,827 MW. The combination is more adverse.

**Calibration rule refinement**: The surplus concentration index captures systemwide solar-vs-NL balance. A secondary modifier is needed: when GR_WEST trough is >4,000 MW, reduce P(WEST_TO_NORTH binding) by one categorical step even if surplus index is high. For 2026-05-31 at 4,537 MW, this modifier reduces the call from VERY HIGH to HIGH — which is what was applied.
