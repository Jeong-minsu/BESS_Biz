## 5. Key Improvement Points — Stage 0 Heuristic Failures Exposed

**1. West wind collapse does not equal West surplus**

The near-zero West wind (348-600 MW) was interpreted as removing the West wind buffer while solar (28.5 GW) dominated. The actual nodal result suggests the system may have managed the surplus without creating binding export congestion at a level that affected GKS's nodal price. Possible cause: West solar may have been more distributed or curtailed, or the PTDF relationship between the West export constraint and GKS node is weak. The heuristic (near-zero wind + high solar = HIGH West binding) was too strong an inference.

**2. Shadow price vs DA-RT spread are different phenomena**

The forecast targeted RT binding congestion with a shadow price in the $20-60/MWh range. The actual outcome was DA-RT spread congestion ($10-15/MWh DA premium). These are related but different: DA binding clears at a higher DA price; RT may or may not replicate. For GKS strategy, the DA-RT spread was more important than RT nodal shadow price. The model needs to distinguish between DA-side congestion (higher DA clearing) and RT-side congestion (real-time nodal deviation).

**3. Timing precision for WEST_TO_NORTH was wrong**

The forecast centered on HE09-14. The actual data showed near-flat prices in that window. There was no midday congestion event detectable at GKS.

**4. Evening congestion timing was accurate (HE20-22)**

The call of HE19-22 was close — HE19 was flat, HE20-22 was the event. This timing precision (within 1 hour) is reasonable for a Stage 0 heuristic. Retaining this pattern for Stage 1.
