## 5. One Improvement for Next Cycle

**Add RT imbalance sign analysis to the self-review template.**

The Tenaska settlement data contains a clear signal: at hours where the battery consumed in RT (RT Cons MWh > 0) and also had a large DA sell position, the RT settlement amount (RT_Energy_Amt) represents the imbalance between DA scheduled generation and RT actual consumption. The sign and magnitude of this imbalance, relative to the DA price, is a proxy for the GKS nodal RT price direction versus DA. This can be computed from existing Tenaska data without any new data sources.

Specifically: at HE10-14, GKS had DA buy positions (negative DA Energy amounts) and RT credits (positive RT Energy amounts). This means RT prices at GKS were above the DA prices during those hours — which directly contradicts a "negative MCC at GKS HE10-14 (RT below DA)" reading, OR indicates the imbalance settlement is on the consumption side (the battery is paying RT to buy energy not in DA, i.e., a load imbalance, not a generation imbalance). The sign conventions in Tenaska settlement for combined DA/RT BESS positions require careful disambiguation.

**Action**: Create a sign-convention interpretation guide for Tenaska Battery-Settlement-Details at `memory/congestion-analyst/plans/tenaska-settlement-sign-conventions.md`. This will allow future self-reviews to extract GKS implied nodal RT price direction from Tenaska data alone, without waiting for hub LMP data.
