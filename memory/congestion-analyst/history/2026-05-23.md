## Headline

Three congestion themes for 2026-05-23:

1. **West export constraint binding, HE09-13 (solar + wind trough anti-correlation)**: Solar peaks at ~20k MW HE13-14. Enverus wind STPF drops sharply from 14,736 MW overnight to a midday trough of 2,849-2,873 MW at HE11-13 — a 12 GW overnight-to-midday wind swing. The concurrent solar build (~16k-20k MW HE10-13) drives ERCOT net load to its daily minimum of 28,729-29,268 MW at HE12-13. The WEST-to-NORTH 345 kV export path faces its highest binding risk during this window. Smartbidder P(DA<RT) is dominant HE00-13 (0.60-0.65), consistent with West zone DA prices being bid down below expected RT.

2. **Evening load ramp / Houston-South import constraint, HE18-21**: Enverus net load surges from 39,928 MW at HE17 to a peak of 49,593 MW at HE20 — a 9,665 MW ramp in three hours — as solar collapses and total wind has only partially recovered (7,361-7,857 MW). ERCOT load itself peaks at 60,706 MW at HE17 and holds above 56k MW through HE21. DA prices spike to $37-44/MWh. Smartbidder P(DA>RT) is strongest HE18-23 (0.61-0.66), indicating DA market priced in import constraint on South/Houston paths. This is the primary shadow price signal of the day.

3. **Overnight West wind ramp-down / early morning residual surplus, HE01-06**: Enverus wind STPF starts at 14,736 MW at HE00 and ramps down to 8,643 MW by HE06. Yes Energy West wind (GR_WEST) is 8,146 MW at HE01 declining to 5,486 MW at HE06. West export path has moderate surplus overnight but less severe than midday solar scenario.
