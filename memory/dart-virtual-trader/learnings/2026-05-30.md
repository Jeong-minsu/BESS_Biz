## 3. Key Decisions Assessed

### 3.1 HE18 First Entry at Half-Size — Refine Rule Going Forward

The 2026-05-28 cycle broke the HE18 skip rule by entering at MED 25 MW. The 2026-05-29 cycle repeated this at half-size. The 2026-05-31 cycle reverses to skip.

**Issue**: The HE18 entry criterion has been applied inconsistently across three cycles:
- 2026-05-27 (for 2026-05-28): skip
- 2026-05-28 (for 2026-05-29): MED half-size 25 MW, P = 58.7%
- 2026-05-30 (for 2026-05-31): skip

This inconsistency needs resolution. Proposed rule (implemented going forward):
- HE18 HALF-SIZE ENTRY (25 MW MED): P(DA>RT) >= 62% AND spread >= $4/MWh AND day-type is normal weekday
- HE18 SKIP: P < 62% OR spread < $4 OR day-type is weekend/holiday

Applying this rule retroactively: The 2026-05-29 HE18 had P = 58.7% and spread = +$6.33. P is below the 62% threshold, which would have triggered a skip. The 2026-05-29 cycle entered anyway — this was a process violation of the proposed rule. For 2026-05-31 (Sunday holiday), the skip is correct under both P < 62% and day-type = holiday criteria.

### 3.2 HE23-24 Thin Spread Risk — Inversion Warning

The 2026-05-31 Smartbidder file shows HE24 RT > DA (inverted spread). This is the first time an overnight hour has shown an inverted spread in any Smartbidder file reviewed. It signals that overnight liquidity and wind dynamics can produce unexpected inversions.

For 2026-05-29 HE23-24: the positions were entered with thin +$1.89 spread and P = 68.6%. Even if the direction was correct, the realized P&L would be small (~$25-50 per position at 25 MW). The process question is whether thin-spread overnight positions (spread < $2) are worth the operational overhead of a small expected P&L.

**Proposed rule**: Skip positions where E[gross] (raw) < $30 per position, even when P clears HIGH tier. For HE23-24 on a holiday weekend (thin liquidity), this would trigger a skip.

Applied for 2026-05-31: HE23 E[gross] raw = ~$32 (marginal), HE24 skipped for inverted spread. The $30 minimum E[gross] rule would have eliminated both, consistent with the skip decision.

### 3.3 P Values Across Friday (HE19-22): Highest Yet

HE21 P(DA>RT) = 76.0% was the highest single-hour P value issued by the model. HE19-22 ranged 73.1-76.0%. These are above the previous peaks (68-71% in earlier cycles).

The high P values on Friday reflect the strong Friday holiday dynamics (commercial early setback, strong wind recovery, solar similar to weekday). If settled with confirmed wins, this would validate the P escalation approach on Fridays vs normal weekdays.

Observation for future cycles: P(DA>RT) appears to be structurally higher on Fridays and days immediately before holiday weekends (when commercial demand setback amplifies the DA > RT pattern). Consider a +2-4pp Friday adjustment to P estimates when Smartbidder file is absent.

### 3.4 Node Choice HB_NORTH for HE16-17 vs HB_HOUSTON
