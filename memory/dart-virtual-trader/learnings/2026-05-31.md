## 3. Key Decisions Assessed

### 3.1 HE13 Entry: Minimal Spread Threshold Challenge

HE13 at +$0.95 raw spread is the lowest-spread entry in this series. The position was taken at MED 25 MW because P=67.6% cleared the HIGH tier. However, the E[gross] at 25 MW × $0.95 × 1.20 (bias) × 0.67 (P-weight) = ~$19. This is BELOW the $30 minimum E[gross] threshold established in the 2026-05-27 learning.

**Finding**: The HE13 entry violated the $30 minimum E[gross] rule. The 2026-05-29 learnings established this filter for overnight thin-spread positions, but it was not applied here to the midday HE13 position.

**Action for 2026-06-01 cycle**: The $30 minimum E[gross] filter applies to ALL positions regardless of hour, not just overnight. The 2026-06-01 cycle correctly skips HE15-16 (spread ~$1.50-1.80) under this reasoning. HE17 at 25 MW × $2.10 × 1.20 × 0.70 = ~$44 barely clears the threshold — included at MED.

**Rule clarification**: E[gross] = MW × E[spread_raw] × bias_correction × P(win). Minimum: $30 per position. Applied universally across all hours starting 2026-06-01 cycle.

### 3.2 HE22 HIGH 50 MW at Thin Spread — Sizing Conflict

The rule matrix specifies: HIGH tier = P >= 60% with E[|spread|] supporting 50 MW. At HE22 on Sunday with spread of +$1.58, the expected gross at 50 MW = 50 × $1.58 × 1.20 × 0.67 = ~$63. This exceeds the $30 minimum per MW but is low in absolute dollar terms and the thin spread creates high volatility (the realized outcome could swing from +$200 to −$200 depending on actual spread).

**Finding**: The HIGH tier (50 MW) sizing for a thin-spread position is questionable. The position cleared the $30 minimum threshold but the spread volatility at $1.58/MWh means the P-weighted outcome is small and the loss scenario (if RT slightly exceeds DA) is equally sized.

**Proposed refinement**: For positions where P >= HIGH but E[spread] < $3/MWh, cap size at 25 MW (MED) rather than 50 MW (HIGH). The expected dollar outcome does not justify 50 MW when the spread is below $3. Exception: the hour is part of a confirmed structural block (HE19-21) where the adjacent hours have large spreads — in that case, the block logic permits maintaining HIGH sizing to avoid partial-block fragmentation.

**HE22 applies the exception (evening block continuity)** — adjacent HE21 has +$6.43 spread. However, going forward, if HE22 spread is <$3 AND HE21 spread is also <$3, the HE22 position should downsize to 25 MW.

### 3.3 HE18 Skip on Sunday — Rule Confirmation

The 2026-05-29 learnings formalized the HE18 entry rule: P >= 62% AND spread >= $4 AND normal weekday. The 2026-05-31 cycle skipped HE18 (Sunday holiday). This is the correct application. Sunday HE18 P was estimated at 64.6% (above threshold) but day-type = holiday weekend triggers the mandatory skip. Outcome: if HE18 RT was softer than DA on Sunday (likely given holiday load suppression), the skip was conservative but appropriate — the spread would have been close to the forecast +$2-3/MWh, generating minimal P&L regardless.

**Rule confirmed**: HE18 weekend/holiday skip rule is maintained.
