This means GKS had essentially no DA sale at HE20 physically, but Smartbidder's DART virtual book would have had a SHORT DA position. If RT settled near $36 and DA was $45.65 (our bid price), then DA > RT and SHORT DA wins. However, the physical picture is inconsistent — if GKS injected 39.194 MWh at RT and received $1,428, the implied RT price is approximately $36.46, and DA was $45.65. Spread = DA-RT = $45.65 - $36.46 = +$9.19. SHORT DA would WIN at this settlement.

**Revised reading for HE20**: The large RT injection at $36-37/MWh while DA was $45.65 suggests HE20 may actually be a WIN for SHORT DA (DA was expensive vs RT realization). The earlier "LIKELY LOSS" designation was based on a misread of the RT_Energy line — high RT Energy Amt reflects large MWh × price, not necessarily high price. With DA at $45 and RT at ~$36, SHORT DA wins.

**Corrected HE20 outcome**: LIKELY WIN. Spread = +$9.19 (above our $7.03 forecast). This is a stronger outcome than forecast.

**Corrected hit rate (known outcomes)**: 5 of 5 known-direction positions appear to be wins. Consistent with Smartbidder DART virtual benchmark of +$1,807.
