## 3. Key Decisions Assessed

### 3.1 Midday Block Direction Reversal (HE10-13): LONG DA → SHORT DA

**This was the highest-stakes process decision of this cycle.**

Prior four cycles used LONG DA at HE10-13 (P(DA<RT) = 58-66%). The 2026-05-28 cycle flipped to SHORT DA (P(DA>RT) = 55-58%) based on Smartbidder file returning and showing DA > RT for the first time at these hours. The structural argument was that 2026-05-28 has much stronger solar (30,118 MW vs 20,425 MW prior day) creating DA > RT even during the solar peak, because the DAM is now also pricing in the high solar availability and bidding down DA.

**Assessment: STRUCTURALLY REASONABLE but the margin is thin**

The P = 55-58% for the SHORT DA midday block is the lowest-conviction P values in any position taken to date. At these levels, the WEST_TO_NORTH binding probability (30-40%) is a serious counterforce. The expected gross for the entire three-hour midday block was only $16 — meaning even if all three hours win, the contribution is negligible. The risk: all three lose if WEST_TO_NORTH binding dominates (loss = 3 × 25 MW × ~$2/MWh = ~-$150, small but directionally wrong).

**Process improvement identified**: When Smartbidder P(DA>RT) for midday block is in the 55-58% range (borderline MED) AND the congestion-analyst flags 30-40% constraint binding probability for the same hours, these two uncertainties should be treated as multiplicatively increasing the skip probability. The current matrix rule only looks at P threshold (>55% → MED eligible), but does not weight down when congestion is high and in the adverse direction. This cycle exposed a gap: the model admitted positions at P = 55.5% facing 30-40% binding probability in the adverse direction — that is functionally a much lower effective P.

**Proposed rule addition**: When P(DA>RT) is in MED zone (55-60%) AND congestion-analyst reports adverse-direction binding probability > 25%, treat as LOW conviction (skip). Apply this to both LONG DA and SHORT DA midday blocks.

### 3.2 HE21 Bid Cap at $75.00 (DA = $89.18)

The cap was set at $75.00 vs prior cycles' $65 cap rule (DA > $70 → $65 cap). This represented a $10 increase in the cap level. The stated rationale was that the 2026-05-28 cap target should be higher because the RT forecast ($55.05) gave more buffer.

**Assessment: Acceptable but cap escalation warrants monitoring**

The $75 cap vs $89.18 DA leaves a $14.18 buffer below the full DA market. However, P(DA<RT) = 49.8% means the model is nearly indifferent on direction relative to the DA market price. Setting a cap at $75 means the position wins if RT < $75 (not just if RT < $89.18). The correction for using $75 vs $65 is: the wider RT forecast buffer ($55.05 vs $73.28 prior cycle) justified a slightly higher cap. But this is a slippery slope — caps should be calibrated to the risk of RT spike exceeding a threshold, not to the size of the expected spread.

**Proposed clarification**: The cap rule should be anchored to P(RT > cap) < 15% rather than to spread buffer. If Smartbidder RT forecast = $55.05 and historical spread at HE20-21 is roughly ±$20-30 at 2σ, then P(RT > $75) ≈ 15-25%. A more conservative cap would be $65-70. The $75 cap was not inappropriate but was at the upper boundary of acceptable for this hour.
