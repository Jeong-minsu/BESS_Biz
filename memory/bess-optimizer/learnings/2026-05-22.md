**What worked well**:

- Non-Spin AS identification was clean: one product dominated (Non-Spin $12-14) vs noise from ECRS ($4.53) and RRS ($2.20) at the same hours. The co-optimization choice (70 MW energy + 30 MW Non-Spin at HE20-21) is quantifiably superior to either pure energy or pure Non-Spin at those hours.
- The overnight charge (HE02-04) at 50 MW spreads risk: avoids oversized single-hour charge commitment when RT prices are uncertain overnight.

**What could be improved**:
- **No AS playbook prior**: recommend_as_position.py does not exist yet. This forces full manual AS product identification from price forecasts alone. Acceptable for now, but the playbook would provide a systematic prior to pressure-test against.
- **SoC accounting was handled narratively, not numerically in the output**: A formal per-hour SoC table (MWh) would make the recommendation more auditable and catch constraint violations (e.g., discharging more than available SoC).
- **RegDown offer at full SoC**: The physical constraint (100% DoD, no headroom to absorb) was flagged but not resolved. Need an explicit rule: if SoC >= 180 MWh, cap RegDown offer at 10-15 MW max.
- **No Tenaska HSL check**: The recommendation assumes HSL = 100 MW (nameplate). Per agent spec, HSL should be confirmed daily via Tenaska PTP `dataPoints=["HSL"]`. This was not done (Tenaska PTP fetch script not running). Flag for operations team.
