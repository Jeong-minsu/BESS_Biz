## 2. Biggest Forecast Misses

### Miss #1 — HE21 DA Price Overestimate (~$21/MWh)

**Forecast**: Smartbidder DA HE21 = $45.65/MWh.
**Actual (implied)**: ~$24/MWh (from GKS Tenaska DA_Energy_Amt at HE21 with ~100 MW position).

**Likely cause**: The 2026-05-22 day (Friday, late-May) had both high solar output (27.5 GW peak) and wind recovery post-sunset. The Smartbidder model may have priced in a tighter reserve margin during the evening ramp than actually materialized. Additionally, this was a late-May weekday where actual load may have run slightly below the 57,265 MW BIDCLOSE average — the actual GKS behavior (charging through HE10-HE14 heavily, discharging HE19-HE21) suggests market conditions were softer than forecast in the evening peak.

**Impact on GKS**: The DA discharge at HE21 earned ~$24/MWh rather than the implied $45+ — a meaningful shortfall. However, GKS still captured value at HE21 (best revenue hour of the day).

### Miss #2 — ECRS Timing (HE23-HE24 vs HE20-HE22)

**Forecast**: ECRS elevated HE20-HE22 ($5.43-$7.24/MWh).
**Actual**: ECRS cleared at HE23-HE24 ($349.30 + $77.00 for GKS), with HE20-HE22 showing RRS clearing instead ($56.70 + $184.10 + $333.20).

**Likely cause**: Wind ramp-back post-sunset at HE23-HE24 (GR_WEST forecast 8,942-13,985 MW) created supply adequacy concerns into the early overnight, driving ECRS procurement later than the DA model anticipated. The briefing correctly identified that wind would recover overnight (HE23 ERCOT COPHSL 13,985 MW forecast) but did not translate this into ECRS procurement timing implications.

**Impact on GKS**: GKS captured ECRS at HE23-HE24 which was not fully anticipated, so this was a positive surprise in execution; the planning signal was off but the outcome was favorable.

## 4. One Change for the Next Briefing

**Improve implied price estimation from P&L data**: The biggest gap this cycle was the inability to directly compare Smartbidder DA forecast prices against realized ERCOT SPP prices. The Tenaska settlement data provides DA_Energy_Amt and MW positions, which can be used to back-calculate the implied settled price (DA_Energy_Amt / DA_scheduled_MWh = implied SPP). This back-calculation should be formalized as a step in the self-review process. Specifically: extract GKS DA Energy hours with non-trivial positions (>10 MW), divide settlement amount by scheduled MW, and record as the "realized SPP proxy" for those hours. This gives a mechanically comparable benchmark against the Smartbidder forecast and allows proper MAE calculation at the peak-price hours — without waiting for an external SPP data feed.
