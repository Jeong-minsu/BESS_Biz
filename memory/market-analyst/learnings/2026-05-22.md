## 4. Process Observations

**What worked well in today's cycle**:
- All five data sources present: Yes Energy BidClose (CSV + summary JSON), Smartbidder (Energy + DA-RT + DA Ancillary + RT Ancillary), Enverus (load, net_load, wind STPF, solar STPF), AG2 (weather, windcast, load_regions, load_subzones). This closes the workflow gap flagged in the 2026-05-21 learning.
- Enverus solar and wind STPF are on interval_start convention (HE1 = 00:00-01:00 local) — consistent with Yes Energy HourEnding convention once adjusted.
- AG2 windcast file contains multi-day data. For 2026-05-23, the ERCOT Aggregate rows begin at line 26 and run 00:00-23:00 CDT.
- RT Ancillary file remains empty of actual values as expected (pre-DAM); DA Ancillary Forecasted columns are populated and usable.
- Smartbidder DA Ancillary Non-Spin shows a clear step-change at HE20 ($12.52) and HE21 ($13.79), confirming the evening peak AS structure.

**What could improve**:
- AG2 load_regions.csv is 634 KB — too large to read in full. Need a summary step or targeted extraction (grep ERCOT Aggregate rows for target date only) before market-analyst reads it.
- Enverus net_load column is labeled `load_mw` but is actually net load (different from gross load). This naming is confusing — should be noted in shared/config.md.
- The AG2 windcast uses "Local Valid Time" in CDT; ERCOT market uses CPT (which is CDT in summer). No conversion needed now, but should be flagged for winter when CDT/CST diverge.
- Solar forecast divergence between Enverus and Yes Energy (+4.2GW at peak) is large enough to materially affect midday net load and RT price. Should flag this to bess-optimizer as a charge opportunity uncertainty band.
